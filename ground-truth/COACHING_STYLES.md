# Coaching Styles

## Architecture

Render deterministic explanations from structured facts:

```text
position facts + classification evidence + pattern evidence
+ personal history + selected style = explanation
```

A future local model may implement `ICoachingProvider`, but factual claims remain grounded in C++ data.

## Beginner and friendly

Plain language, one lesson, minimal notation, supportive without fake praise.

> Your bishop was attacked. Moving another pawn left it undefended, so Black can win it. Before each move, check what your opponent attacked.

## Cynical and hard

Direct, concise, unforgiving about the decision, never abusive toward the person.

> You ignored an attacked bishop to play a pawn move that solved nothing. Black takes it and the position collapses. Check threats before inventing plans.

## Devil's advocate

Challenges assumptions and asks for the opponent's forcing replies before revealing the conclusion.

> You want to play d5. What happens after the center opens while your king is uncastled? List Black's checks, captures, and threats first.

## Settings

Style, depth, question-first mode, auto-reveal, notation level, and future audio.

## Rules

Critique moves, not intelligence or character. The hard voice may be sharp, not degrading. Never invent psychology or certainty beyond the evidence.

Release evidence

Personal Chess Tutor

A local-first C++20 systems project that turns a player’s own Chess.com games into progressive Stockfish analysis, explainable mistakes, interactive drills, spaced repetition, and long-term weakness metrics.

Systems depth

Subprocess IPC, isolated worker pools, prioritized bounded queues, cooperative cancellation, crash recovery, checksummed binary events, migrations, snapshots, atomic compaction, bounded caches, profiling and packaging.

Correctness

Legal move generation, FEN/SAN/PGN, perft, make/unmake properties, deterministic replay, two board representations, fault injection and end-to-end load tests.

Product judgment

Responsive shallow-first analysis, reserved interactive capacity, raw metric denominators, evidence-linked recommendations, local privacy, and independently validated optional puzzles.

Measured tradeoffs

Two workers selected as the conservative default. Candidate bitboards and mmap rejected where measurements did not justify complexity.
